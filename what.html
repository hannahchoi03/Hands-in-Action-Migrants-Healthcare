Who are Migrant Workers?

A migrant worker is someone who leaves their country for better chances at employment. They usually do not intend to stay in the new region. Though many immigrants are well educated and earn high salary jobs, poorly educated and have no choice but to go to low wage factories that do not provide sanitary nor safe working conditions.

Why is this a problem?

Immigrant workers are often exploited because they are more desperate for a job than native workers and thus have less negotiating power. This leads to poor working conditions, disregard of legal rights, and lower wages for the same task.

Their rights

Their trip to their designated country is sponsored by the owner of the company, which gives the owners full control of the workers' actions/wellbeing during their time in Korea. In other words, the workers can not quit their jobs or move to other factories of their own will since their living requirements (housing, etc.) are in the hands of the employers and they would have to get the government's approval in order to do so. This places undocumented immigrants in a worse position since the employers could easily expose their presence in the country.
